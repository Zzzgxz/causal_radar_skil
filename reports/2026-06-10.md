### 背景/问题
Long chain-of-thought (CoT) trajectories in large language model (LLM) reasoning cause severe inference bottlenecks due to rapid key-value (KV) cache growth. Current decoding-time compression methods mitigate this issue via token eviction, but typically assume a uniform budget distribution across all layers and heads.

### 方法
Long chain-of-thought (CoT) trajectories in large language model (LLM) reasoning cause severe inference bottlenecks due to rapid key-value (KV) cache growth. To bridge this gap, we propose ReasonAlloc, a training-free framework that recasts decoding-time KV compression as a hierarchical budget allocation problem.

### 实验/数据
ReasonAlloc operates at two complementary levels: an offline layer-wise preallocation strategy captures an architecture-driven demand pattern which we call ``\textit{Reasoning Wave}'', while an online head-wise strategy reallocates resources during decoding to information-rich heads based on real-time utility.

### 结果
